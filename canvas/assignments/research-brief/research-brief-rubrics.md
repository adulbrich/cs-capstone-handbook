# Research Brief - Rubrics

## Source quality and citation (20 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (20 to >16.0 pts): 3–5 credible sources; proper citation/links; distinguishes claims vs. facts.
- **Meets Expectations** (16 to >4.0 pts): ≥3 sources with basic citation.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (4 to >0 pts): <3 sources or unclear citations.

## Synthesis and implications (30 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (30 to >24.0 pts): Synthesizes findings into 2–3 concrete implications for team approach.
- **Meets Expectations** (24 to >6.0 pts): Summarizes findings with at least one implication.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (6 to >0 pts): Lists facts without implications.

## Accuracy and relevance (20 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (20 to >16.0 pts): Findings are accurate and directly relevant to the project scope.
- **Meets Expectations** (16 to >4.0 pts): Mostly accurate/relevant with minor issues.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (4 to >0 pts): Significant inaccuracies or off-topic content.

## Writing organization and mechanics (15 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (15 to >12.0 pts): Clear headings; brief; no grammar barriers; figures/tables labeled if used.
- **Meets Expectations** (12 to >3.0 pts): Understandable with minor issues.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (3 to >0 pts): Hard to follow or error-prone.

## Communication clarity (15 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (15 to >12.0 pts): Explains technical terms; uses visuals or examples effectively.
- **Meets Expectations** (12 to >3.0 pts): Explains most terms; visuals optional.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (3 to >0 pts): Jargon-heavy; unclear audience targeting.
