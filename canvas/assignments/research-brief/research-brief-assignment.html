Individual Submission

Purpose

  • Build a quick, accurate understanding of a narrow domain/tech/market slice relevant to your project.
  • Practice finding and judging credible sources, separating fact from opinion.
  • Synthesize what you learned into concrete implications your team can act on.
  • Communicate clearly to mixed technical/non-technical teammates.

Task

Deliverable, Audience and Genre: One-page research brief for your capstone teammates and instructor; assume a mixed audience (technical + stakeholder).

Allowed Resources: University library databases, Google Scholar, standards bodies, reputable industry research, official vendor docs, your own exploratory measurements, and Generative AI tools for idea generation, editing, organization, and phrasing. Do not fabricate evidence (e.g., hallucinated sources).

Check the handbook's Requirements activities if you need a starting point (e.g., fill in the lean canvas and back your claims with data, research, or testing). Each team member could focus on a different activity/slice of the project.

  1. Pick a focused slice tightly tied to your project (e.g., a library/framework decision, a regulatory constraint, a user segment, or a competitor pattern). State the slice in one sentence at the top. 
  2. Find 3–5 credible sources (e.g., peer-reviewed papers, reputable industry reports, vendor docs with known authorship, standards bodies, government data, benchmarks).
  3. Read and distinguish facts from claims/opinions; note where numbers come from.
  4. Synthesize the key takeaways (not a list of summaries). Convert them into 2–3 concrete implications for your team’s approach (design, risks, scope, tooling, metrics, or next experiment).
  5. Draft the brief with clear headings; explain terms and acronyms on first use; use one small visual/table only if it clarifies.
  6. Add an accessible reference list with working links (any consistent citation style).
  7. Do a quick peer pass using the prompts in Self-Check before submitting.

Deliverables and Format

Submit your Research Brief (one page; markdown or PDF)

  • Filename: YYYY-MM-DD-[ONID]-ResearchBrief.pdf/md (e.g., 2025-09-23-ulbrical-ResearchBrief.pdf)
  • Contents (suggested structure):
    • Updated Project Description or Problem Statement (2–4 sentences)
    • Slice & Why it Matters (1–2 sentences)
    • Key Findings (synthesized)
    • Implications for Our Team (2–3 bullets)
    • Evidence & References (second page)
  • Evidence to include (place on a seperate page):
    • citations/links;
    • glossary;
    • brief quotes or data points with source;
    • (optional) one labeled figure/table.

Formatting note: The research brief is strictly one page. Include all evidence and references on a separate second page within the same file.

Define acronyms; label any figure/table; keep visuals accessible (alt text or caption).

Quality Checlist

  • Is the topic narrow and clearly tied to the project scope?
  • Do I have 3–5 credible, traceable sources with working links?
  • Did I separate facts from claims/opinions and cite where numbers come from?
  • Does the writing synthesize across sources (not a list of summaries)?
  • Are there 2–3 specific, actionable implications for our team?
  • Is content accurate and relevant; are technical terms explained; is organization clean and skimmable?
  • If I used a visual, is it necessary, readable, accessible, and labeled?
  • Is anything inaccurate, outdated, or off-scope? (Fix or flag limits.)
  • What is the clearest action the team should take from this brief, and what’s missing to justify it?
  • Which statement most needs a citation or source quality upgrade?
  • Where could synthesis replace summary? Propose the synthesis.
  • Identify any jargon or ambiguous term and propose a plain-language alternative.

See the Canvas rubric for detailed performance levels.

Reflection (optional, ~5–8 min)

  • What changed in my team’s plan because of this brief? What quick test or conversation should we run next?
  • Which source types were most useful for this domain, and where should we look next time?
