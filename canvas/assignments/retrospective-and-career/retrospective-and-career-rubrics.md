# Retrospective and Career - Rubrics

## Individual reflection: project insights and personal growth (17 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (17 to >13.6 pts): Reflection is specific and personal; identifies a concrete owned area, a specific skill developed (with an example), a genuine challenge and how it was addressed, and something underestimated at the start that the student now understands well; goes meaningfully beyond the team retrospective.
- **Meets Expectations** (13.6 to >3.4 pts): Reflection is present and mostly personal but some sections are vague, restate the team narrative, or lack concrete grounding.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (3.4 to >0 pts): Reflection is generic, primarily restates the team narrative, or is missing key elements (owned area, specific skill, challenge, or growth).

## Contribution evidence: concrete artifacts tied to owned area (14 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (14 to >11.2 pts): 2&ndash;3 artifacts provided with links or references; each has a 2&ndash;3 sentence caption explaining what it is, what problem it solved, and what it demonstrates about the student's skills; artifacts are specific to the student's work and could credibly be discussed in an interview.
- **Meets Expectations** (11.2 to >2.8 pts): At least one artifact is provided with a caption; relevance to the student's owned area is partial or captions lack specificity.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.8 to >0 pts): Artifacts are missing, inaccessible, or not meaningfully tied to the student's individual contributions.

## Resume: capstone project section quality (23 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (23 to >18.4 pts): Capstone project entry includes project title and dates, a one-sentence project summary (including the project outcome if it is notable), 3&ndash;5 impact-oriented bullets using action verbs and quantified outcomes where possible, a concise tech stack tag line, and a link to the project; bullets describe the student's personal contributions and accomplishments; overall resume is consistently formatted and proofread.
- **Meets Expectations** (18.4 to >4.6 pts): Capstone entry is present and includes most required elements; bullets may be duty-focused rather than impact-focused, or some elements (dates, link, tech stack) are missing.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (4.6 to >0 pts): Capstone section is missing from the resume, or the entry is a placeholder with insufficient detail to be useful in a job application.

## Career narrative: connection between project and career goals (17 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (17 to >13.6 pts): Thoughtfully connects the capstone experience to the student's career direction; identifies specific skills to build on; acknowledges honestly if the project revealed a gap or changed direction; states one concrete thing to do differently on the next team, grounded in the capstone experience.
- **Meets Expectations** (13.6 to >3.4 pts): Career narrative is present and mostly connected to the project; some parts are vague or aspirational without grounding in specific capstone experiences.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (3.4 to >0 pts): Career narrative is missing or generic ("I want to work in software engineering and this project helped me improve").

## Peer resume review: specificity and helpfulness (11 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (11 to >8.8 pts): Feedback addresses all four required areas (capstone entry, resume readability, career narrative, one concrete suggestion); comments are specific enough that the peer could act on them; tone is professional and collegial; the concrete suggestion identifies a clear change and explains how to make it.
- **Meets Expectations** (8.8 to >2.2 pts): Feedback is present and mostly relevant but some areas are skipped, comments are vague, or the concrete suggestion lacks enough detail to be actionable.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.2 to >0 pts): Peer review is missing, too brief to evaluate, or consists only of surface-level praise without substantive feedback.

## Writing: professionalism and clarity (12 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (12 to >9.6 pts): Reflection document and resume bullets are professionally written; claims are precise and verifiable; no typos or grammatical errors; resume bullets use consistent action verb formatting.
- **Meets Expectations** (9.6 to >2.4 pts): Mostly professional; minor typos or formatting inconsistencies; some bullets drift from action-oriented to duty-oriented.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.4 to >0 pts): Unprofessional writing; significant typos or errors; resume bullets are passive, duty-focused, or unprofessional.

## Writing: accessibility (6 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (6 to >4.8 pts): Screenshots and artifacts include alt text and captions; links use descriptive text; private artifacts include a screenshot or excerpt for grading; resume is ATS-friendly (no tables, text boxes, or multi-column layouts that break parsing).
- **Meets Expectations** (4.8 to >1.2 pts): Some accessibility practices used; minor gaps (missing alt text, vague link text, or resume format may have minor ATS issues).
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.2 to >0 pts): Missing alt text or captions; evidence not evaluable due to inaccessible links; resume in a format likely to fail ATS parsing.
