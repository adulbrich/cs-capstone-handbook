# Progress Report - Rubrics

## Progress against plan: quantification and plan linkage (10 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (10 to >8.0 pts): Completed vs. planned work is clearly quantified; progress is explicitly tied to the team’s plan/sprint goals.
- **Meets Expectations** (8 to >2.0 pts): Accomplishments and next steps are listed; some quantification or plan linkage is present.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2 to >0 pts): Updates vague; no clear connection to the plan.

## Progress against plan: traceability links (8 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (8 to >6.5 pts): Every major claim is supported with links to artifacts/issues/PRs/commits.
- **Meets Expectations** (6.5 to >1.5 pts): Many claims include links, but coverage is incomplete.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.5 to >0 pts): Few or no links.

## Progress against plan: blockers and replans (7 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (7 to >5.5 pts): Blockers are documented with concrete replans (what changed, why, and what’s next).
- **Meets Expectations** (5.5 to >1.0 pts): Blockers are mentioned with basic next steps.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): Blockers/replans are missing or unclear.

## Supporting artifacts: per-item evidence coverage (12 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (12 to >9.5 pts): Each planned item in “Progress vs. Plan” has at least one verifiable supporting artifact, or a single artifact clearly covers multiple items.
- **Meets Expectations** (9.5 to >2.5 pts): Many items have supporting artifacts, but coverage is incomplete or doesn’t clearly map to the listed items.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.5 to >0 pts): Few or no items have verifiable supporting artifacts.

## Supporting artifacts: relevance and context (7 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (7 to >5.5 pts): Artifacts are clearly relevant to claims and include brief context (what it shows and why it matters).
- **Meets Expectations** (5.5 to >1.0 pts): Artifacts are mostly relevant but context/captions are inconsistent.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): Artifacts are unclear, unlabeled, or not clearly tied to the claims.

## Supporting artifacts: traceability and “version of truth” (6 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (6 to >4.8 pts): Artifacts link to an evaluable source of truth. For code-related artifacts, commit hash(es) are included; for write-ups, a stable link plus title/date/version/location is included.
- **Meets Expectations** (4.8 to >1.0 pts): Some traceability details are present, but hashes/links/context are incomplete.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): Artifacts aren’t traceable (no hashes where applicable, broken links, or missing context).

## Risk and quality tracking: risks/bugs logged and current (8 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (8 to >6.5 pts): New risks/bugs are logged and the list is current (not stale).
- **Meets Expectations** (6.5 to >1.5 pts): Risks/bugs are mentioned but tracking is incomplete.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.5 to >0 pts): Risks/bugs not tracked.

## Risk and quality tracking: ownership, status, and actions (6 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (6 to >4.8 pts): Each major risk has an owner and status; clear actions assigned.
- **Meets Expectations** (4.8 to >1.0 pts): Some owners/status/actions are present.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): Ownerless or actionless tracking.

## Risk and quality tracking: quality signals and trend (6 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (6 to >4.8 pts): Quality signals (blockers, bugs, tests, CI failures, etc.) include a trend or comparison over time.
- **Meets Expectations** (4.8 to >1.0 pts): Some quality signal is reported without a clear trend.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): No quality signals.

## Writing: structure and readability (5 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (5 to >4.0 pts): Concise, well-structured, polished; uses clear headings and bullets; images/media enhance readability.
- **Meets Expectations** (4 to >1.0 pts): Mostly clear with some structure; minor readability issues.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): Hard to read; poorly structured.

## Writing: professionalism and clarity (5 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (5 to >4.0 pts): Professional tone; claims are precise and easy to follow.
- **Meets Expectations** (4 to >1.0 pts): Generally clear; occasional ambiguity or awkward wording.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): Unclear or unprofessional tone.

## Writing: accessibility (5 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (5 to >4.0 pts): Images/media are legible and include alt text and short captions; links are descriptive.
- **Meets Expectations** (4 to >1.0 pts): Some accessibility practices are used; minor gaps.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): Missing alt text/captions or inaccessible formatting.

## Team process reflection: specificity and insight (7 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (7 to >5.5 pts): Reflection is specific and grounded in what happened; identifies the “why,” not just the “what.”
- **Meets Expectations** (5.5 to >1.0 pts): Reflection present but somewhat general.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): No meaningful reflection.

## Team process reflection: actionable improvements (5 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (5 to >4.0 pts): 1–2 concrete, actionable improvements with clear next steps.
- **Meets Expectations** (4 to >1.0 pts): Improvements listed but may be vague.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.0 to >0 pts): No actionable improvements.

## Team process reflection: next-sprint application (3 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (3 to >2.4 pts): Shows how the team will apply improvements next sprint (e.g., who/when/how tracked).
- **Meets Expectations** (2.4 to >0.5 pts): Intends to apply improvements but plan is light.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (0.5 to >0 pts): No plan to apply improvements.
