# Spring Release - Rubrics

## Problem statement: clarity and directness (16 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (16 to >12.8 pts): Opens with a direct, specific answer to "What problem are you solving?"; states the problem clearly in one or two sentences; names who experiences it and what it costs them; briefly explains why prior solutions were insufficient; and defines the team's specific scope. Concrete throughout, no vague framing.
- **Meets Expectations** (12.8 to >3.2 pts): Problem statement is present and mostly clear but some elements are vague, the cost or consequence to the user/system is underspecified, or the team's scope is not well-defined.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (3.2 to >0 pts): Problem statement is missing, generic, or not clearly connected to the showcase.

## Project showcase: completeness, realism, and polish (27 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (27 to >21.6 pts): Shows the project in action in a format appropriate to the project type. Product/tool teams: starts from a realistic user entry point and walks through one or two complete use cases end-to-end; narrated clearly. Research/pipeline teams: walks through the pipeline or results end-to-end; shows the system in action; presents key findings with context for a non-specialist audience. All teams: technically interesting choices are highlighted; no long silences; features or limitations not fully working are honestly noted.
- **Meets Expectations** (21.6 to >5.4 pts): Showcase shows the project working but may skip a complete end-to-end walkthrough, include long silences or unclear narration, or present results without sufficient context for a general audience.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (5.4 to >0 pts): Showcase is absent, uses only slides, is too short to evaluate the work, or does not show the project or pipeline in action.

## Outcomes: results, impact, and honest assessment (20 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (20 to >16.0 pts): Clearly states what was delivered vs. original goals; provides evidence of impact or validation (user testing, partner feedback, performance data, research findings, etc.); shares 1&ndash;2 meaningful lessons; identifies the highest-priority next step with a specific reason; tone is honest and professional, not promotional or defensive.
- **Meets Expectations** (16.0 to >4.0 pts): Outcomes are summarized but scope comparison is superficial, evidence of impact is absent, or lessons are vague.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (4.0 to >0 pts): Outcomes segment is missing or claims only success without honest assessment of scope or limitations.

## Delivery: clarity, pacing, and professionalism (16 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (16 to >12.8 pts): Well-paced narration throughout; transitions between segments are clear (verbally or with title cards); supporting visuals (slides, diagrams, or annotated screen content) are present for non-showcase segments and complement the narration without being read verbatim; technical or research content is explained at a level appropriate for a mixed audience; professional tone; recording feels polished and intentional, not improvised.
- **Meets Expectations** (12.8 to >3.2 pts): Mostly clear delivery; some pacing issues (too fast, too slow, or long silences); transitions between segments may be unclear; supporting visuals may be absent for some segments or read from directly; level of explanation may not suit a mixed audience.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (3.2 to >0 pts): Hard to follow; unclear narration; significant pacing issues; no supporting visuals for non-showcase segments; tone is unprofessional or the recording feels unfinished.

## Recording: technical quality, length, and hosting (13 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (13 to >10.4 pts): Recording is 8&ndash;12 minutes; video and audio are clear (1080p recommended; no background noise; legible screen content); uploaded to media.oregonstate.edu as unlisted with a working link; timestamps for each segment are included in the release notes; recording link was tested and works at submission.
- **Meets Expectations** (10.4 to >2.6 pts): Recording is mostly within length; audio or video quality has minor issues; timestamps may be approximate or missing from the release notes; link is accessible.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.6 to >0 pts): Recording is significantly over or under length; poor audio/video quality; link is broken, inaccessible, or not on media.oregonstate.edu; no timestamps.

## Release notes: completeness and accuracy (8 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (8 to >6.4 pts): One-page PDF includes project name, team ID, date, working media.oregonstate.edu link with segment timestamps, one-paragraph summary, bulleted features/findings list, known limitations, and a link to the repository or archive.
- **Meets Expectations** (6.4 to >1.6 pts): Release notes present and mostly complete but missing some elements (e.g., timestamps, limitations, or archive link).
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1.6 to >0 pts): Release notes missing or too sparse to be useful.
