Team Submission with Individual Contributions

Purpose

  • Explain the term through a clear narrative of patterns, not a status list.
  • Support claims with lightweight, concrete artifacts.
  • Commit to realistic team-level and individual-level improvements.
  • Strengthen shared ownership of engineering practice and team health.

What You Must Produce

Submit one team retrospective (PDF, 1-2 pages plus individual pages). Individual grades depend on demonstrated reflection quality and specificity.

  • Filename format: YYYY-MM-DD-[TeamID]-Retrospective.pdf
  • Example: 2026-03-15-CS.042-Retrospective.pdf

Required Report Structure

Your submission must include the following sections. Each section corresponds directly to a rubric criterion.

1. Team Narrative

Write a synthesized story of your term (consolidated from individual write-up + discussion), not a list of events. Focus on team dynamics, how you worked together, and what patterns helped or hurt (not just technical output). Include:

  • Top 3 successes and top 3 pain points
  • Brief, plausible causes (what contributed to each success/pain point)
  • What you learned about how your team functions (communication, coordination, decision-making, accountability, workload balance, inclusion, etc.)
  • Short references to concrete examples (links or screenshots) that support your claims

2. Evidence Examples 

Provide lightweight artifacts that reinforce your narrative (1-3 examples). Evidence can be technical or team/process-focused (choose what best supports your story). Examples include:

  • Sprint board snapshots (showing flow, bottlenecks, or churn)
  • Issue or PR links (with 1–2 sentence captions)
  • Before/after PR or code review excerpts (if relevant)
  • Small screenshot of a discussion thread (legible) showing decision-making, coordination, or conflict resolution
  • Meeting agenda/notes, decision log excerpts, team agreement excerpts, or retro action tracking (if your team uses them)
  • Peer feedback or check-in notes (redact sensitive info as needed)

If a link is private, include a small screenshot or quoted snippet so graders can evaluate it.

3. Team Action Plan

List at least three team actions you will implement next term. For each action, include:

  • Owner (single person responsible)
  • Timeframe (e.g., “by Week 3”, “starting next sprint”)
  • Plain-language recognition cue describing how you will know it’s working
    (e.g., “standup handoffs <5m”, “review turnaround usually same day”)

4. Individual Pages

Attach clearly labeled individual pages (~1 page per student). For each student, include:

  • 1–2 wins and 1–2 gaps with at least one concrete example tied to your owned area.
  • Two improvement commitments that are realistic, actionable, and checkable, each including:
    • How progress will be checked (date/event/cue)
    • The observable outcome you expect
  • One likely blocker you will personally take the lead on preventing next term. Think of this as: “What is one thing that could block you or the team next term, and what will you personally do about it?” For this blocker, include:
    • A clear mitigation step (what you will actually do)
    • A simple trigger for when to act (e.g., “if review queue >5 PRs”)
    • A “helped” cue (how you will know your mitigation had impact)

Formatting Guidelines

  • Team sections ~1-2 pages; individual pages attached after.
  • Use clear headings, bullets, and descriptive link text.
  • Include alt text for images; use short captions for artifact snippets.
  • No dashboards or analytics exports—keep evidence lightweight.
  • Generative AI may be used for writing. Do NOT fabricate artifacts or data.
