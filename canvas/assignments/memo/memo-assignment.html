Individual Submission

While this is an individual submission assignment, you are expected to meet with your project partner or mentor as a team. We want each student to first look at the problem and project from their own individual perspective.

Purpose

  • Practice professional information-gathering and note-taking with an external stakeholder.
  • Translate the discussion into a clear problem statement, constraints, and expectations.
  • Discover and address IP, licensing, and data-use considerations early to avoid rework.
  • Communicate findings concisely in a one-page technical/business memo.

Task

Deliverable, Audience and Genre: A one-page internal memo to your project team and instructor that captures project partners intake discussion evidence and decisions.

Allowed Resources: calendar/meet tools, note-taking/transcription (optional), style/grammar aids, team repo/docs, and Generative AI tools for idea generation, editing, organization, and phrasing. Do not fabricate evidence (e.g., fake citations).

Check the handbook's Creative and Planning activities to get started. Depending on your situation and your stakeholder(s), some of these activities might be more useful than others. 

  1. Plan the project partner intake session. Draft goals and 6–10 questions; align with your team. Obtain consent to record, if applicable. Take notes.
  2. Lead or co-lead the discussion with your project partner. Capture direct quotes or timestamped paraphrases. Note consent/recording status.
  3. Synthesize the problem & constraints. Write a clear problem statement plus initial constraints/acceptance criteria derived from the intake session.
  4. Identify IP/licensing & data-use items. Note who owns what (code, content, data), any expected license(s), and privacy/security implications; include immediate next steps (e.g., “confirm with sponsor whether GPL is acceptable”).
  5. Capture project partner expectations. Document deliverables, success measures, communication cadence, availability/SLAs, tools, and any “must/must-not” items; confirm via a brief follow-up email. Please discuss the six facets you will be evaluated on at the end of the term.
  6. Write the memo. One page, scannable headings/bullets, working links, professional tone.
  7. Package and submit. Include supporting evidence listed below. Keep sensitive details out of public repos; pseudonymize if needed.

If your project is student-led, please discuss these items as a team, then review your discussion with your project mentor. In that case, quotes can be taken from any students on the team or from the project mentor.

Deliverables and Format

Submit your Memo file (markdown or PDF)

  • Filename: YYYY-MM-DD-[ONID]-Memo.pdf/md (e.g., 2025-09-23-ulbrical-Memo.pdf)
  • Contents:
    1. Quick Facts (stakeholder, date, consent/recording status if you record)
    2. Evidence (5–10 quotes or timestamped paraphrases)
    3. Problem Statement (2–4 sentences)
    4. Initial Constraints & Acceptance Criteria (bullets)
    5. IP/Licensing/Data-Use (ownership, intended license(s), privacy/security notes, next steps)
    6. Stakeholder Expectations (deliverables, measures, comms cadence, SLAs/availability)
  • Evidence to include (place on a separate page):
    • Project Partner intake plan outline (goals + questions)
    • Notes/transcript excerpt or summary with timestamps
    • Follow-up confirmation email (PDF/screenshot or pasted text)
    • Links to any partner terms/OSS licenses referenced

Formatting note: The rmemo is strictly one page. Include all evidence and references on a separate second page within the same file.

Use clear headings, bullets, and hyperlinks; note consent and attributions; avoid proprietary details in the memo—link to private storage if required by sponsor.

Quality Checklist

  • Did you plan a purposeful intake discussion with a brief plan, usable notes, and 5–10 grounded quotes/timestamps, with consent/recording status noted?
  • Does your problem statement reflect stakeholder language and include concrete initial constraints/acceptance criteria you can test?
  • Have you correctly identified early IP/licensing/data considerations and listed realistic next steps?
  • Are stakeholder expectations explicit (deliverables, measures, cadence, availability) and verified via a short confirmation email?
  • Is the memo one page, skimmable, link-checked, and professional; do grammar/style not impede meaning?
  • What’s the strongest piece of evidence? What key decision lacks evidence?
  • Are any constraints or expectations ambiguous? Rewrite one for testability.
  • Do IP/licensing/data notes suggest any risks the team should mitigate this term?

See the Canvas rubric for detailed performance levels.

Reflection (optional, ~5–8 min)

  • What did you learn about the stakeholder’s real success criteria? What will you verify next?
  • Which constraint or IP/licensing item could block progress later? What’s your mitigation plan?
