# Project Landing Page - Rubrics

## Content: project description and value proposition (20 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (20 to >16.0 pts): One-sentence description is clear and specific; the problem being solved and why it matters are communicated concisely for the target audience; the &ldquo;so what?&rdquo; is compelling and non-generic; content is written for a real audience, not for a grader.
- **Meets Expectations** (16.0 to >4.0 pts): Description and value proposition are present but may be vague, technically-focused rather than audience-focused, or written primarily for an evaluator rather than a real audience.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (4.0 to >0 pts): Project description is missing, too vague to understand what the project does, or reads like a course deliverable rather than a real page.

## Key features and visual demonstration (20 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (20 to >16.0 pts): 3&ndash;5 features, findings, or capabilities described from the audience&rsquo;s perspective (not the implementation perspective); at least one screenshot, screen recording, figure, or embedded demo shows the project in action or illustrates results; media is legible, purposeful, and directly illustrates the claims made.
- **Meets Expectations** (16.0 to >4.0 pts): Features or findings are listed; media is present but may be low quality, off-topic, or fails to clearly demonstrate functionality or results.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (4.0 to >0 pts): Features or media are missing; or content is described only in technical terms with no audience framing.

## Call to action and how to access (13 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (13 to >10.4 pts): A clear, prominent call to action (Download, Play, Try it, View on GitHub, Read the paper, etc.) is present and functional; prerequisites and platform limitations are briefly noted; the path from the page to actually engaging with the project is obvious.
- **Meets Expectations** (10.4 to >2.6 pts): A call to action exists but may be buried, non-functional, or missing important prerequisites or limitations.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.6 to >0 pts): No clear call to action; or the link is broken; or it is not clear how to access or engage with the project.

## Visual design and polish (20 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (20 to >16.0 pts): Page looks intentional and professional; visual hierarchy guides the reader; typography, spacing, and layout are consistent; the page would not be embarrassing to share in a job interview or with a real user; platform conventions are followed appropriately.
- **Meets Expectations** (16.0 to >4.0 pts): Page is functional and readable but feels unpolished or like a default template with minimal customization.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (4.0 to >0 pts): Page appears unfinished, uses broken layout, or looks like a rough draft.

## Team credits and contact (13 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (13 to >10.4 pts): All current team members are credited; past contributors are acknowledged if this is a multi-year project; GitHub or portfolio links are included for each member (or deliberately omitted with a stated reason); a contact or feedback channel is present.
- **Meets Expectations** (10.4 to >2.6 pts): Team credits are present but some members are missing, past contributors are unacknowledged on a multi-year project, or no contact channel is provided.
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.6 to >0 pts): Team credits or contact information are missing.

## Accessibility (14 pts)

- **Exceeds Expectations** (14 to >11.2 pts): All images have alt text; color contrast is sufficient for readability; links use descriptive text; videos or GIFs have captions or a written description; the page is navigable without relying solely on color or visual layout.
- **Meets Expectations** (11.2 to >2.8 pts): Some accessibility practices are used; minor gaps (e.g., a few images missing alt text, or link text could be more descriptive).
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** (2.8 to >0 pts): Missing alt text throughout; insufficient color contrast; or the page is not meaningfully accessible.
