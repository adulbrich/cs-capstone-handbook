Individual Submission

This is the first low-stakes peer-review evaluation worth 25 points. You have the exact same survey to complete at the end of the term, worth 100 points. Please use this assignment's grade to course-correct, if needed. Consider reading conflict resolution activities. This assignment is only 5% of your grade and you cannot earn less than half credit (minimum 12.5/25).

Purpose

  • Ensure individual accountability and fairness in team grading.
  • Practice giving specific, professional feedback to peers.
  • Reflect on effective team behaviors you want to repeat (or avoid) in future projects.

What You Must Produce

Complete the Qualtrics survey sent to your OSU email inbox. There is nothing to submit in Canvas.

Refer to the handbook for more information on the criteria and grade calculation.

The survey is open for a limited time only. A failure to submit by the due date will result in 12.5/25.

If you have questions about your grade after results are processed, contact the instructors directly. Your responses are anonymous to peers and shared with instructors only. TAs might be made aware of certain dynamics.

Survey Structure

Roster Confirmation

Open the Qualtrics link sent to your ONID email and confirm your team roster in the survey. We use ONID usernames (e.g., ulbrical@oregonstate.edu) instead of first and last names.

Criteria Ratings

Rate each teammate (including yourself) on the different criteria.

100-Point Allocation

Distribute 100 points across all team members (including yourself).

  • The total must equal 100.
  • You must assign yourself at least 100/N points (N = team size).

Qualitative Comments

Optional but strongly encouraged: add comments supporting unusually high or low allocations and noting specific helpful or harmful behaviors.

Guidelines

  • Ratings reflect observed behaviors and artifacts (tasks completed, PRs reviewed/merged, meeting reliability), not popularity.
  • The 100-point split is honest and justified, not uniform by default nor punitive without evidence.
  • Comments (when provided) include specific examples (e.g., “closed 7 issues on API auth; fixed flaky tests; led sprint demo”) or specific gaps (e.g., “missed 3 standups; 2 PRs required major rework due to failing tests”).
  • Tone remains professional and constructive; focuses on work and impact.
  • All team members (including self) are rated on all criteria, and totals are valid (sum = 100; self ≥ 100/N).
  • Generative AI may be used for writing. Do not fabricate artifacts, evidence, or data; all examples and claims must be real.
